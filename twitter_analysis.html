Twitter Data Analysis

Elections entitles citizens to choose their President. Democracy means government for the people and by the people. During the 2016 November presidential elections, public views towards the future leader fluctuated quickly as interviews, response to world events and other issues were addressed by the top election candidates like Donald Trump, Hillary Clinton, Bernie Sanders and Ben Carson. Twitter provides us with access to views about the elections across the globe. We analyzed recent tweets regarding the top candidates in 2016 presidential elections and tried to predict public’s sentiments towards each candidate and opinions and emotions towards the elections.

Our Approach

We focused on tweets with reference to the presidential elections and its candidates. We downloaded over 900,000 tweets based off recent tweets and assessed the sentiments. Our Keywords were “Donald Trump”, “Hillary Clinton”, “Politics”, “elections”, “political candidates”, etc.

Data Source: Using Python and Twitter API, we built a crawler that downloaded the tweets.

Process Text: Our project analyzed the feelings of Twitter’s younger, tech-savvy audience. The tweets sometimes contained words like “be”, “at”, “the” etc. that do not express any sentiment. So, we had to linguistically process the data.

Data Analysis: MongoDB and R programming was used to analyze the Twitter Data.

Analysis of Maps: Using the entry for latitude - longitude coordinates and user-inputted locations, we showed the concentration of tweets from each city relative to the total number of tweets we have about each candidate.
