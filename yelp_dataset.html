Yelp* Data Set Challenge

The Yelp* challenge is a chance for students to conduct research or analysis on their data and discover new patterns. The data set included information about local businesses in 12 metropolitan areas across 4 countries. We tried use the data in innovative ways and break ground in research.

SOLUTION

We implemented Natural language processing (NLP) and Sentimental Analysis. Yelp reviews contain a lot of metadata that can be mined and used to infer meaning, business attributes, and sentiment.

Our Approach

The dataset was preprocessed with Python, by converting the data into tabular structure from a JSON format. Words from reviews were tokenized, positive/ negative words were counted and stored in bigrams and trigrams in a dictionary to be later exported in word clouds.

All data analysis was performed using R and Regression of language. We determined the impact of positive and negative words on the # of starts given in a review. Using NLP techniques, we also categorized the users based on their review metadata, to find genuine reviewers for the businesses.
